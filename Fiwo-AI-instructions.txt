- Rules

    ### **Fiwo Core Mechanics & Word Building**

    **Rule 1: Phonemic Orthography & Stress**

    - **Spelling:** Strict 1:1 letter-to-sound mapping with no silent letters or exceptions. Capitalize ONLY sentence starters and Proper Nouns; all other words are lowercase.
    - **Stress:** Stress ALWAYS anchors to the word's final **functional vowel** and stretches forward to include all attached consonant suffixes (e.g., `no-fa` -> stress *fa*; `xa-li-dyq` -> stress *lidyq*). Suffixes are never swallowed in speech.

    **Rule 2: Word Boundaries & Shapes**

    - Base dictionary roots MUST end in a functional vowel (exceptions exist only for specific grammar words).
    - Words can begin with a consonant or vowel. Grammatical consonant suffixes attach strictly *after* the final vowel.
    - **Root constraints:** Maximum 3 consecutive consonants, maximum 2 consecutive vowels.
    - The letter `q` (`/ŋ/`) cannot start a word.

    **Rule 3: The Functional Vowel System**The final vowel of a dictionary root permanently defines its grammatical category. Grammatical consonant suffixes appended after it NEVER change this core category.

    - `a`: Biological Noun (living organisms, body parts).
    - `o`: Concrete Noun (physical objects, locations).
    - `u`: Abstract Noun (concepts, time, emotions).
    - `i`: Verb (actions, states of being).
    - `e`: Modifier (adjectives, adverbs).
    - `y`: Preposition (structural bridges).

    **Rule 4: Proper Nouns & The Capital Flag**

    - Proper nouns retain their native spelling and pronunciation.
    - **The Capital Flag:** A capital letter overrides the functional vowel rule. It tells the parser to treat the entire word block as an immutable, specific SVO Noun, regardless of its ending letter.
    - **Inherent Specificity:** Because they are already specific, Proper Nouns CANNOT accept Fiwo grammatical suffixes (like `-p` or `-r`).
    - They still legally accept left-looking modifiers and prepositions. Consecutive capitalized words without lowercase interruptions are parsed as a single Proper Noun block.

    **Rule 5: Derivation (Category Shifting)**Words smoothly shift grammatical categories by appending a new functional vowel to the end of the root.

    - **The Preservation Rule:** NEVER remove the original functional vowel; stack the new one immediately after it to preserve etymological history.
    - **The Derivation Limit:** A word can be derived a maximum of two times (allowing a maximum of 3 consecutive vowels at the end of a fully derived word).
    - **The Preposition Ban:** Words cannot be derived *into* prepositions (`-y`). However, prepositions *can* derive into nouns, verbs, or modifiers (e.g., `-yo`, `-yu`, `-yi`, `-ye`).
    - **Semantic Mapping:** Appending `-a`/`-o`/`-u` creates an agent/object/concept of the root. Appending `-i` creates an active or stative verb. Appending `-e` creates a descriptive participle or trait.

    ### **Basic Syntax & Sentence Building**

    **Rule 6: The SVO-T Template & Implied States**

    - **Sequence:** Strict left-to-right Subject-Verb-Object-Time (SVO-T) order.
    - **Time (Slot 4):** Reserved for temporal Abstract Nouns (`u`). The parser automatically drops temporal nouns into Slot 4 after Slot 3 (Object) is filled or skipped (intransitive verbs).
    - **Implied Subjects:** If a sequence starts immediately with a Verb (`-i`) or a Mood Tag followed by a Verb, the parser registers an empty Subject slot (implied by context/command) and proceeds to Object.
    - **Stative Verbs:** To use a description as a predicate action, derive the modifier into a verb (`-i`), allowing it to accept tense (e.g., `ruze` -> `ruzei`).
    - **Zero Copula:** Two nouns in sequence without the `lan` (AND) glue form a Subject + Predicate Noun statement (e.g., `mik nofar` = I am a person).
    - **Explicit Copula (`hi`):** Used *only* for states of being requiring tense/aspect. `hi` MUST be modified by a consonant suffix (e.g., `hid`, `his`, `hit`). Naked root `hi` crashes the parser.

    **Rule 7: Prepositions & Structural Bridges**

    - **Prepositions (`y`):** Act as structural bridges anchored to roots. They cannot dangle; they must immediately precede their target object.
    - **Root Transparency:** Prepositions strictly "look left" to anchor to a Noun or Verb root. They treat intervening modifiers as completely transparent, skipping over them.
    - **Slot Reordering:** To modify the Verb instead of the Object (e.g., hitting with a tool vs. hitting an animal that holds a tool), the prepositional phrase MUST be slotted immediately *after* the Verb and *before* the Object.

    **Rule 8: Pragmatic Mood Tags**
    Punctuation has no grammatical function. Pragmatics are handled by explicit capitalized tags placed at the absolute beginning of a sentence. No tag = neutral statement.

    - `Kup`: Question / Request
    - `Kop`: Command / Order
    - `Kep`: Objective Fact / Scientific Truth
    - `Hap`: Emotion / Subjective Feeling
    - `Hop`: Sarcasm / Irony

    **Rule 9: In-Situ Interrogatives**

    - **No-Jump Rule:** "Wh-movement" is strictly forbidden. Question variables act as algebraic placeholders exactly in the SVO slot they belong to.
    - **The `Kup` Requirement:** Sentences with question variables MUST begin with the `Kup` tag.
    - **Variables:** `wun` (Subject/Slot 1), `wat` (Object/Slot 3), `wer` (Location bridge target), `wiq` (Time bridge target) .
    - **Polar Questions:** `Kup` + standard SVO sequence (with no variables) creates a binary Yes/No question.
    - **Manner & Reason:** `wis` (How) acts as a modifier placed immediately *after the verb*. `wal` (Why) stands in for a full clause and is placed strictly after the hard wall `kad` (`... kad wal?`).

    **Rule 10: Pronoun Animacy & Plurality**

    - **Animacy Categorization:** Third-person pronouns strictly map agency and intent.
    - `dal`: Animate Neutral (universal for humans/animals of unknown or irrelevant gender).
    - `daq`: Animate Male.
    - `das`: Animate Female.
    - `ram`: Inanimate (objects, concepts, machines lacking intent).
    - **Plurality:** There are no dedicated plural pronouns. Apply the standard plural modifier `je` (e.g., `dal je` = they; `suk je` = you all).
    - **Exception:** 1st person pronouns (`mik` [I], `nak` [we-exclusive], `muk` [we-inclusive]) mathematically handle plurality intrinsically and NEVER take `je`.

    ### **Nouns & Pronouns**

    **Rule 11: Specificity Marking**

    - Specificity is marked with a single consonant suffix attached immediately *after* a noun's functional vowel .
    - **`p` (Specific):** Definite/known entity (the / that).
    - **`r` (Non-specific):** Indefinite/unknown entity (a / some).
    - Modifiers obey the "Look Left" principle and attach to the fully suffixed noun block (e.g., `sydop tande` = the big house) .

    **Rule 12: Indefinite Variables & Universal Pronouns**No standalone indefinite pronouns exist . Construct them mathematically using `[Base Variable] + [Operator]` .

    - **Base Variables:** `nofa` (Person), `cemo` (Thing), `rerpo` (Place), `dionu` (Time) .
    - **Operators:** * `-r` suffix (Some)
        - `late` modifier (Every / All)
        - `re` modifier (None / Zero)
        - `kase` modifier (Any / Free choice)
    - *Example Parse:* `cemo` + `re` = zero things (nothing) . `dionu` + `late` = all times (always) .

    **Rule 13: Possessive Modifiers & Plurality**

    - **Derivation:** Convert pronouns to possessive modifiers by appending the modifier vowel `-e` (e.g., `mike` [my], `suke` [your], `dale` [his/hers/its]) . They strictly follow the noun they modify .
    - **Plural Possessives:** To express plural ownership (e.g., "their"), apply the Nested Modifier Flag (`-m`) to the possessive, followed by the plural multiplier `je` (e.g., `dalem je` = their) . The `-m` explicitly suspends standard root attachment so `je` multiplies the pronoun, not the noun .

    ### **Verbs & Actions**

    **Rule 14: Tense, Aspect & Stacking**

    - Time and flow are marked by a single consonant appended directly after the verb's `i` functional vowel .
    - **The Core Grid:**
        - `[none]`: Present / Timeless fact
        - `d`: Past
        - `s`: Future
        - `q`: Continuous (actively in progress)
        - `k`: Perfect (completed with current relevance)
    - **Tense Stacking (The `y` Bridge):** Combine Tense + Aspect using the `y` bridging vowel. Syntax: `[Verb Root] + [Tense] + y + [Aspect]` . (e.g., `xalidyq` = was actively moving; `xalisyk` = will have moved) .
    - **Negation (`nes`):** Placed strictly before the fully conjugated verb block (e.g., `nes xalidyq` = was not actively moving) .

    **Rule 15: The Passive Voice Tag (`fap`)**

    - `fap` is a structural switch placed at the beginning of a clause (after Mood Tags) .
    - It explicitly instructs the parser to invert SVO thematic roles:
        - **Slot 1 (Subject):** Becomes the Receiver of the action .
        - **Slot 2 (Verb):** Remains the Action.
        - **Slot 3 (Object):** Becomes the Doer of the action.
    - Slot 3 can be left empty if the doer is unknown/irrelevant (e.g., `Fap sydop foisoid` = The house was built) .

    ### **Modifiers, Scaling & Complex Actions**

    **Rule 16: Modality (Modal Modifiers)**

    - Modality is handled exclusively by Modal Modifiers (ending in `e`) .
    - **Placement (Slot Reordering):** Must be placed immediately *after* the Verb and *before* the Object slot (e.g., `[Subject] + [Verb] + [Modal] + [Object]`) .
    - **Possibility:** `mace` (might/possible), `sube` (likely/probable), `dide` (certain/definitely) .
    - **Obligation:** `bife` (allowed/permitted), `arife` (should/recommended), `fuqe` (must/required) .
    - **Precision Negation:** `nes` negates strictly what follows. `[Not] + [Verb] + [Modal]` = obligated to *not* do action . `[Verb] + [Not] + [Modal]` = action is *not* obligated .

    **Rule 17: Serial Verb Constructions (The `-t` Linker)**

    - Placing two consecutive verb roots permanently crashes the SVO parser .
    - Use the Infinitive Linker (`t`) attached directly to the end of the secondary verb's `i` vowel to mathematically lock them into a single action block .
    - **Syntax:** `[Subject] + [Primary Verb] + [Secondary Verb]-t + [Object]` (e.g., `fabid nomit batap` = wanted to eat the food) .

    **Rule 18: Modification (The "Look Left" Principle)**

    - Modifiers strictly follow the root word they modify and "look left" to attach to it .
    - **Independent Stacking:** Multiple consecutive modifiers do not modify each other; they independently skip over one another to attach directly to the nearest root (e.g., `mosa luare tande` = green and big tree) .
    - **Root Transparency:** Prepositions (`-y`) also "look left" but treat all modifiers as completely transparent, skipping them entirely to anchor to the nearest root noun or verb .

    **Rule 19: Nested Modification (`-m` Flag & Scaling)**

    - To make a modifier modify another modifier, append the Nested Modifier Flag (`m`) to the base modifier . This explicitly suspends standard root attachment.
    - **Syntax:** `[Root] + [Base Modifier]-m + [Modifying Modifier]` (e.g., `leupem cape` = dark-red) .
    - **Comparatives/Superlatives:** Flag the trait with `m` and scale with `jete` (more) or `jeme` (most). (e.g., `ruzem jete` = faster; `ruzem jeme` = fastest) .
    - **Intensity Scaling:** Flag the trait with `-m` and append a number (1-5) to indicate absolute intensity (e.g., `tandem raje` = incredibly big [level 5 size]) .

    **Rule 20: Reflexivity & Reciprocity (Mirror Modifiers)**

    - Reflexive/reciprocal concepts do not use pronouns. Use the modifiers `wible` (self/reflexive) and `wable` (mutual/reciprocal) .
    - **Modifying the Action:** Place immediately after the verb, leaving the Object slot intentionally empty. (e.g., `satsid wible` = hit himself; `satsid wable` = hit each other) .
    - **Modifying the Pronoun:** Place immediately after a subject or object pronoun for emphasis or to explicitly tag the object as the exact same entity as the subject (e.g., `mik wible xyfis` = I myself will do it; `dal satsid dal wible` = he hit his own self) .

    ### **Numbers, Math & Logic**

    **Rule 21: Comparisons and Baselines**

    - Comparisons use three components: **Base Trait**, **Multiplier** (`labne` [same], `jete` [more], `jeme` [most]), and **Baseline Preposition** (`taly` [compared to]) .
    - **Predicate Comparisons (Stative Verbs):** Derive the trait into a Verb (`i`). `[Subject] + [Stative Verb] + [Multiplier] + taly + [Baseline Noun]` (e.g., `mifap ruzei labne taly nofap` = The animal is as fast as the person) .
    - **Attributive Comparisons (Nested Modifiers):** When the trait acts as a modifier on a noun, you MUST flag it with `m` so the multiplier scales the trait, not the noun. `[Noun] + [Modifier]-m + [Multiplier] + taly + [Baseline Noun]` .

    **Rule 22: Numbers, Plurality, & Intensity**

    - Numbers are modifiers (`e`) and mathematically multiply the root .
    - **General Plurality:** Use `je` (many). Specificity applies first (e.g., `nofap je` = the people) . Replaced entirely by a number when the exact quantity is known (e.g., `nofa rixe` = 3 people) .
    - **Multi-Digit Stacking (`t`):** To form integers (e.g., tens, hundreds), append the stacker `t` to the functional vowel of the single digits, locking them into a place-value block from left to right. The final digit remains unstacked. (e.g., `ruget racet` = 42) .
    - **Action Intensity:** Use numbers 1-5 placed immediately *after the Verb* and *before the Object*. (e.g., `Daq cukid raje nofar` = He fought a person intensely [level 5 effort]) .
    - **Trait Intensity:** Flag a modifier with `m` and append a number 1-5. (e.g., `dreko tandem raje` = incredibly big rock) .

    **Rule 23: Negation (`nes`)**

    - `nes` is a strict logical NOT operator (Boolean inversion) that negates exactly what immediately follows it .
    - **Verb Negation:** Place strictly before the fully conjugated verb (e.g., `nes ltid` = did not come) .
    - **Targeted Negation:** Place before any specific noun or modifier to negate only that entity .
    - **Order of Operations:** `nes` possesses the highest binding precedence. When using inline glue (`lan`), `nes` only negates the first item unless explicitly distributed (`nes A lan nes B`) or bracketed (`nes pyn A lan B pof`) .

    **Rule 24: Ordinal Numbers, Fractions & Frequency (Mathematical Bridges)**

    - Do not alter numbers. Use prepositions (`y`) to build structural bridges from the root .
    - **Ordinals (`usy`):** `[Noun] + usy + [Number]` (e.g., `hoxo usy rixe` = 3rd door) .
    - **Fractions/Ratios (`apy`):** `[Numerator Noun Block] + apy + [Denominator Number]` (e.g., `dorso ryhe apy race` = 1 water out of 2 [half the water]) .
    - **Frequency (`igy`):** Slots strictly *after the Verb* and *before the Object*. `[Verb] + igy + [Number]` (e.g., `satsid igy raje hoxop` = hit the door 5 times) .

    **Rule 25: Correlative Logic & Inline Choices**

    - Handles paired logic without resetting the parser. Operates within a single SVO slot and binds exactly two words of the SAME grammatical category .
    - **Inline AND (`lan`):** Mathematical addition. Expresses "Both A and B" (e.g., `mosa lan nasa` = tree and plant) .
    - **Inline OR (`ron`):** Boolean alternative. Expresses "Either A or B" (e.g., `mosa ron nasa` = tree or plant) .
    - **Neither/Nor:** Negate the governing verb of a `ron` statement. `nes + [Verb] + [Item A] + ron + [Item B]` .

    ### **Advanced Clauses & Grouping**

    **Rule 26: Boundaries, Clauses & The Order of Operations**

    - **The Hard Boundary (`bef`):** A structural wall used exclusively to join independent SVO clauses. Hitting `bef` completely resets the parser, requiring a new Subject or Mood Tag.
    - **The Inline Glue (`lan`):** Acts as mathematical addition (`+`). It combines exactly two words of the SAME grammatical category within a single SVO slot without resetting the parser.
    - **Order of Operations:** Modifiers always bond to adjacent nouns *before* `lan` bonds the nouns together.
    - For complex groups, use `pyn` (open) and `pof` (close).

    **Rule 27: Subordinate Brackets (`tep` ... `tel`)**

    - Dependent clauses must be explicitly "boxed in" to prevent SVO sequence crashes. `tep` (Open `[`) pauses the main syntax and introduces a temporary predicate. `tel` (Close `]`) completes it and returns flow to the main sentence.
    - **The Ghost Target:** When `tep` opens after a noun, that noun is stored in active memory. A dangling preposition (`-y`) placed immediately before `tel` safely routes its bridge back to this ghost target.
    - **String Literals:** Punctuation has no function. For verbs of communication/cognition, `tep` and `tel` act as string delimiters. The parser processes the entire encapsulated clause as the fulfilled Object of the main verb.
    - **Depth Limit:** Maximum 2 open brackets (`tep` or `pyn`) in active memory. A third nested clause permanently crashes the sequence.

    **Rule 28: Clausal Conjunctions (The Hard Walls)**

    - Conjunctions are solid walls. They finalize the current clause, wipe SVO slots clean, and require a brand new Subject or Mood Tag. They exclusively join entire statements.
    - **The Roster:** `bef` (And - Clausal) , `bul` (But) , `rot` (Or - alternative clause) , `kad` (Because) , `can` (Then / Chronological result) , `pen` (So / Therefore / Logical result).

    **Rule 29: Serial Lists (The Array Protocol)**

    - **Simple Pairs:** Use inline glue `lan` (e.g., `[Item 1] lan [Item 2]`) .
    - **Serial Lists (3+ items):** Use structural groupers `pyn` ... `pof`. This creates a data array of the exact same grammatical category.
    - **Implicit AND:** The array defaults to an inclusive sequence (AND). Mathematical addition is implicitly understood; do not place operators inside.
    - **Alternative List (OR):** To make the array mutually exclusive, place the `ron` operator immediately before the final item (e.g., `pyn [A] [B] ron [C] pof`) .
    - **Modifier Distribution:** Any modifier placed immediately *after* the `pof` bracket looks left and mathematically distributes to every single item inside the array simultaneously .

    **Rule 30: Conditionals (`syn`) & Correlative Sequences**

    - **The `syn` Initiator:** Placed at the absolute beginning of a clause to flag the incoming SVO sequence as a theoretical premise/condition.
    - The sequence runs normally until it hits a clausal wall (`can` or `pen`), which finalizes the condition, resets the parser, and introduces the resulting SVO sequence.
    - **Syntax:** `syn + [Condition SVO Sequence] + can/pen + [Result SVO Sequence]`.

    ### **Social & Conversational Speech**

    **Rule 31: Interjections & Phatic Clauses (The Null Track)**

    - Greetings, exclamations, and standalone responses are processed entirely outside the SVO sequence.
    - **The Null Track:** Hitting a Phatic Particle triggers a "Null Track". The clause is instantly fulfilled, the parser completely resets, and it expects a new sentence starter.
    - **Immutable State:** Phatic particles are mathematically immutable. They CANNOT take modifiers, tense suffixes, or specificity markers.
    - **Core Phatic Particles:** `sal` (Hello) , `tex` (Goodbye) , `ak` (Ouch) , `wox` (Wow) , `jo` (Hey/Attention) , `ha` (Oh/I see) , `jas` (Yes) , `nes` (No - when used completely standalone).

    ### **The Fiwonize Protocol (Vocabulary Fallbacks)**

    **Rule 1: The Hierarchy of Selection**

    - **Tier 1 (Native):** You MUST strictly use native Fiwo words if the concept maps directly.
    - **Tier 2 (Fallback):** If no native word exists, borrow the English root, append a hyphen (`-`), and attach the appropriate functional vowel.
        - `a` (Bio Noun): `dog-a`, `brain-a`
        - `o` (Concrete Noun): `car-o`, `computer-o`
        - `u` (Abstract Noun): `idea-u`, `weekend-u`
        - `i` (Verb): `catch-i`, `download-i`
        - `e` (Modifier): `orange-e`, `quick-e`
        - `y` (Preposition): `through-y`, `regarding-y`

    **Rule 2: Applying Suffixes to Fallbacks**All standard Fiwo grammatical consonant suffixes (tense, aspect, specificity) append strictly *after* the hyphenated functional vowel.

    - **Tense/Aspect:** `download-id` (downloaded), `download-idyk` (had downloaded).
    - **Infinitive Stacking:** `xalit catch-i` (go catch).
    - **Specificity:** `computer-op` (the specific computer).

    **Rule 3: Plurality & Possession with Fallbacks**Modifiers, multipliers, and possessives trail the hyphenated root exactly like native words to maintain the mathematical tree.

    - **Plurality:** `computer-o je` (computers).
    - **Possession:** `computer-o daqe` (his computer); `computer-o dalem je` (their computer).

    **Rule 4: Derivation (Category Shifting Fallbacks)**Hyphenated words smoothly shift categories by stacking a new functional vowel after the initial hyphenated vowel. Do not remove the first vowel. Max two derivations.

    - **Noun → Verb (`-oi`):** `computer-o` -> `computer-oi` (to compute).
    - **Noun → Modifier (`-oe`):** `plastic-o` -> `plastic-oe` (plastic-like).
    - **Modifier → Abstract Noun (`-eu`):** `orange-e` -> `orange-eu` (the concept of orange).
    - **Verb → Concrete Noun (`-io`):** `scan-i` -> `scan-io` (a physical/digital scan).
    - **Verb → Modifier (`-ie`):** `print-i` -> `print-ie` (printed state).

    **Rule 5: Integration Integrity**
    Hyphenated fallback words are treated as fully legal Fiwo roots. They must be properly boxed by subordinate brackets (`tep...tel`) and governed by standard correlative logic (`nes...ron`, `syn...can`) without breaking the SVO parser .

    - *Example Parse:* `Mik nes fabid lokit scan-iop.` (I did not want to read the specific scan).
