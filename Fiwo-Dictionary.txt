Grammar
-a ;; Biological Noun Derivation - Derives a biological noun, such as a living organism, plant, animal, or body part.
-d ;; Past Tense - Indicates an action that occurred in the past.
-dyk ;; Past Perfect - Establishes that an action was completely finished before another past action occurred.
-dyq ;; Past Continuous - Indicates an action that was actively in progress during the past.
-e ;; Modifier Derivation - Derives a descriptive modifier.
-f ;; Distributive Flag - The -f suffix tells the parser to act as mathematical parentheses, distributing the modifier backward across every noun connected by lan until the chain breaks.
-i ;; Verb / Stative Verb Derivation - Derives an action or a stative verb.
-k ;; Perfect Aspect - Indicates a completed past action that has a direct result or relevance right now.
-m ;; Nested Modifier Flag - Signals the parser to suspend the Look Left rule, allowing the next modifier to attach to this word instead of the root.
-o ;; Concrete Noun Derivation - Derives a concrete noun, such as a physical object, material, tool, or location.
-p ;; Specific Noun Marker - Explicitly identifies a known, definite entity.
-q ;; Continuous Aspect - Indicates an action actively in progress at this exact moment.
-r ;; Non-specific Noun Marker - Indicates an indefinite, unknown, or general entity.
-s ;; Future Tense - Indicates an action that will occur forward in time.
-syk ;; Future Perfect - Sets a deadline for when a future action will be completely finished.
-syq ;; Future Continuous - Describes an ongoing state at a specific point in the future.
-t ;; The Stacker - Mathematical linker that locks adjacent words into a single syntactic block, combining single digits into multi-digit integers or permanently chaining serial verbs.
-u ;; Abstract Noun Derivation - Derives an abstract noun, such as a concept, emotion, time, or intangible idea.
ak ;; Ouch / Pain - Exclamation of sudden physical pain or intense discomfort.
ap ;; Divided by / Fraction of / Out of - This is an immutable mathematical operator used to perform explicit division or create fractions within a sentence. When placed between two numerical blocks, the parser evaluates the calculation first, unifying them into a single quantitative value before the result looks left to attach to a noun. It can also be used in a Zero Copula sentence to state a mathematical equation (e.g., sluqe ap dewe tafe = 6 divided by 2 is 3).
bef ;; clause connector / and (clausal) - Clausal wall introducing an independent parallel thought; completely resets the SVO parser.
bip ;; Firstly / First of all - A pragmatic mood tag placed at the absolute start of a sentence to introduce the first point in a sequence.
bul ;; but - Clausal wall introducing a contrasting thought or exception.
can ;; then - Clausal wall introducing a subsequent chronological action; resolves a conditional premise.
cef ;; Please - Expresses politeness, courteous intent, or softens a command.
dal ;; he / she / it (animate) - Third-person singular animate neutral; an entity with intent or agency where gender is unspecified or irrelevant.
daq ;; he / him - Third-person singular animate male; a male entity with intent or agency.
das ;; she / her - Third-person singular animate female; a female entity with intent or agency.
fap ;; Passive Voice Indicator - Structural tag placed at the clause start to mathematically invert SVO thematic roles.
ha ;; Oh / I see - Indicates sudden realization, acknowledgment, or understanding.
hap ;; Emotional / Subjective - An expression of personal feeling or subjective experience.
hop ;; Sarcastic / Ironic - Sarcastic or ironic intent, explicitly encoded to prevent logical misinterpretation.
hyp ;; Hypothetical / Counterfactual - A pragmatic mood tag placed at the absolute start of a clause to indicate that the following statement is a simulation of a reality that does not exist. It translates the English concepts of \
jas ;; yes - Affirmation or agreement.
jo ;; Hey / Attention - Sharp exclamation to command immediate attention or warn.
kad ;; Because / For the reason that - A structural wall that introduces an entirely new independent clause (with its own subject and verb) that explains the reason for the previous clause.
kep ;; Factual / Objective - An objective statement, scientific truth, or absolute fact, strictly excluding everyday observations.
kof ;; Furthermore / Moreover / In addition - A structural particle used to introduce an additional point, fact, or argument.
kop ;; Command / Imperative - A direct order or command.
kup ;; Question / Request - An explicit inquiry or request for information.
lan ;; Plus / And (Inline Additive) - Binds elements of the same grammatical category. Can be chained for lists of three or more.
mik ;; I / me - First-person singular; the speaker
mis ;; Minus / Subtracted by - This is an immutable mathematical operator used to perform explicit subtraction within a sentence. When placed between two numerical blocks, the parser evaluates the calculation first, unifying them into a single quantitative value before the result looks left to attach to a noun. It can also be used as the subject of a Zero Copula sentence to state a timeless mathematical equation (e.g., raje mis dewe tafe = 5 minus 2 is 3).
muk ;; we / us (inclusive) - First-person plural inclusive; the speaker, the listener, and potentially others.
mux ;; Times / Multiplied by - This is an immutable mathematical operator used to perform explicit multiplication within a sentence. When placed between two numerical blocks, the parser evaluates the calculation first, unifying them into a single quantitative value before the result looks left to attach to a noun. It can also be used as the subject of a Zero Copula sentence to state a timeless mathematical equation (e.g., tafe mux dewe sluqe = 3 times 2 is 6).
nak ;; we / us (exclusive) - First-person plural exclusive; the speaker and others, explicitly excluding the listener.
nes ;; no / not - Strict logical operator of negation (boolean NOT); standalone negative interjection.
nop ;; Inferred / Logical Evidence - A pragmatic mood tag indicating the speaker knows the statement is true through logical deduction or physical evidence.
pen ;; so / therefore - Clausal wall introducing a direct result, consequence, or logical conclusion.
pot ;; Decimal Point / Radix - This acts as a structural pivot within a numerical sequence. It explicitly signals to the parser that the whole integers have stopped, and any digits following it are to be read sequentially as fractional values. The parser reads the entire sequence, including the decimal pivot, as a single quantitative block until the final -t stacker is dropped.
pov ;; Meanwhile / At the same time - A structural particle used to introduce an event or action happening concurrently with another.
ram ;; it (inanimate) - Third-person singular inanimate; strictly an object, concept, plant, machine, or entity lacking agency.
ron ;; Or (Inline Alternative) - Inline mathematical boolean OR; offers a choice between elements of the same grammatical category.
rop ;; Reported / Hearsay Evidence - A pragmatic mood tag indicating the speaker knows the statement is true because they were told by someone else or read it somewhere.
rot ;; or - Clausal wall presenting an alternative choice or independent clause.
sal ;; Hello / Greetings - Acknowledges presence or initiates a social interaction.
sek ;; List Separator / Spoken Comma - A structural particle that explicitly separates sequential items in a list or breaks the `-t` stacker bond between adjacent number blocks. It acts as a phonetic wall, telling the parser to finalize the current item and move to the next. Used for enumerating individual multi-digit numbers (e.g., counting `10, 11, 12`) and for listing items without using `lan`. Neutral enumeration (unlike `lan`, which asserts logical AND).
sep ;; In sum / In conclusion - A pragmatic mood tag placed at the absolute start of a sentence to introduce a summary or conclusion.
sil ;; this - Proximal deictic; the current or immediately preceding clause or concept.
sop ;; Direct / Sensory Evidence - A pragmatic mood tag indicating the speaker knows the statement is true because they directly experienced it (e.g., saw, heard, or felt it happen).
suk ;; you - Second-person singular; strictly the listener.
suv ;; You (formal/respectful) - The formal second-person singular pronoun used to show respect when addressing elders, authorities, strangers, or in professional contexts. Possessive form: `suve` (your/yours, formal singular). Pluralized with `je`: `suv je` (you all, formal), possessive `suvem je` (your/yours, formal plural).
syn ;; Condition Initiator - Marks a theoretical premise or conditional clause (if/when/whenever).
tan ;; that - Distal deictic; a conceptually distant, prior, or non-current clause or concept.
tel ;; end of subordinate clause / close bracket - Closing bracket finalizing a subordinate or conditional clause. Optional at sentence boundaries (periods), but strictly mandatory mid-sentence or to resolve mirroring/nesting ambiguity.
tep ;; subordinate predicate marker - Opening bracket introducing a subordinate or embedded relative clause. Open brackets are implicitly closed by a period (.) at the end of a sentence.
tex ;; Goodbye / Farewell - Closes a social interaction or indicates departure.
vax ;; However / On the other hand - A structural particle used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously.
vel ;; While / Whereas - Introduces a completely new, independent clause that occurs simultaneously alongside the previous clause, or presents a direct contextual contrast (whereas).
wal ;; why - Interrogative clause variable for a reason or cause.
wat ;; what - Interrogative variable for a thing or matter.
wer ;; where - Interrogative variable for a location.
wiq ;; when - Interrogative variable for time.
wis ;; how - Interrogative variable for the manner, method, or degree of an action or state.
wox ;; Wow / Awe - An exclamation of positive surprise, wonder, or amazement.
wug ;; How many / How much? (Interrogative) - An interrogative variable specifically holding the place of an unknown numerical quantity or mathematical amount. It sits directly in the SVO slot where the numerical answer is expected.
wun ;; who - Interrogative variable for a person or agent.
xap ;; For example - A pragmatic mood tag placed at the absolute start of a sentence to introduce an example or illustration.
xom ;; In other words / That is to say - A structural particle used to introduce an explanation, clarification, or rephrasing of a previous statement.
zol ;; Although / Even though - Introduces a concessive clause; it acts as a structural wall indicating that the fact in this clause makes the information in the next clause surprising, contrasting, or unexpected.
